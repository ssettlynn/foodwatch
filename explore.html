The Big Patterns

Six charts that explain global hunger

The descriptive analysis behind the models — the same story the notebook's EDA tells. All interactive: hover for details, click legends to hide and show.

1The world was winning — then 2020 happened

Hunger fell for a decade, then COVID-19 and the 2022 price shock erased half the progress.

2Where hunger lives

Sub-Saharan Africa carries the highest burden — and is the only region back near its 2010 level.

3Hunger follows income — structurally

In the poorest group, the typical country sits above the serious-hunger line.

4Money matters — until it doesn't

Each dot is a country (2023). Income cuts hunger fast among the poor, then the effect flattens.

5Which signals move with hunger

Correlation across every country-year. Food-supply adequacy is the strongest partner of hunger (blue = moves together, red = moves opposite). No two inputs are near-identical, so nothing is redundant.

6Importing food isn't the problem

Grouping countries by how much cereal they import shows no clear hunger trend — a genuine null result. The Model Lab's association rules explain why: wealthy import-dependent countries stay food-secure.
